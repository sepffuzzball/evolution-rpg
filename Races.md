# Races
All creatures, monstrous or humanoid have a race. This is hugely important for monsters as they do not get class or jobs except for half-monsters (which can have either a class or a job, but not both).

# Race Statistics
Races receive stat increases each time their race levels up. The point pool is based on character type, the actual tier record the race is assigned to, and rarity, then distributed by the race's stat-growth ratio. The tier used here is not the race template's minimum available tier.

Race level-up points: `Character Type Multiplier * (Tier * 2) * 10 * Rarity Multiplier`.

Character Type multiplier:
* Humanoid: 1
* Half-monster: 2
* Monster: 3

Character type multipliers are managed from the app Compendium's Types tab and are stored with the shared ledger.

Rarity multiplier:
* Common: 1
* Uncommon: 1.5
* Rare: 2
* Epic: 2.5
* Legendary: 3
* Mythical: 3.5
* Divine: 4

# Race Skills
All Races, humanoid or monstrous, get race-specific skills, but there's a vast difference in how many they get or upgrade per tier or level. Because monsters do not receive a class or job, they get a total of 3 racial skills or racial skill upgrades per tier increase, whereas humanoids tend to only get one racial skill or racial skill upgrade per tier increase. Half-monsters split the difference, getting two racial skills or racial skill upgrades per tier increase.

# Example Monstrous Race Progression
* Monsters tend to evolve and branch paths a lot more (like  digimon) than their humanoid counterparts as it's where they get most of their diversity
T01 - Wolf (Common) - Not all T01 monsters are common, but it's certainly the most common
T02 - Lesser Dire Wolf (Common) - If our example wolf did not really stand out but still made it to T02, it's likely they'll get the standard next evolution in the same rarity
T03 - Dire Wolf (Common) - Still at the same rarity, they likely only had a couple options to evolve into and decided to stick with the same track
T04 - Greater Dire Wolf (Uncommon) - T04 monstrous races cannot go below Common rarity as it's uncommon to get to this tier. In this instance, our monster still was pretty 'basic' but managed to level up
T05 - Warg (Rare) - Finally our monster has shown some ambition...whether they stood out with some awesome kills or did something extraordinary, they finally have started to improve their race rarity and managed to evolve into a Warg
T06 - Frost Warg (Rare) - Most likely during T05 our Warg did stuff in a cold climate...whether that was eating a powerful frost witch or using the cold to their advantage, the System made note enough to give them the option to evolve into something with their chosen element.
T07 - Avalanche Lycan (Epic) - Our monster is still pacing one tier above the average, making it to Epic tier. Clearly the system has recognized even more of their cold abilities.
T08 - Lycan of the Frozen Moon (Epic) - Tier 08 monsters are powerful in their own right, especially those that are a full tier ahead of the curve. 
T09 - Calamitous Night Lycan (Legendary) - Our monster gains a Domain and becomes a true legend.
T10 - Lunistara (God of the Moon) - Ascended to Godhood.
